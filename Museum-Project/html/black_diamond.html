The Enigma Beneath the Surface

Black diamonds, also known as carbonado diamonds, stand apart from all other gemstones found on Earth. Unlike traditional diamonds that dazzle with transparency and brilliance, black diamonds absorb light, holding within them the silent history of extreme pressure, immense heat, and unimaginable time.

Origins: Forged Under Extreme Conditions

The formation of black diamonds challenges conventional geological understanding. While white diamonds crystallize under stable mantle conditions, black diamonds are polycrystalline ‚Äî composed of millions of tiny diamond crystals fused together.

Internal Structure & Composition

Unlike transparent diamonds, black diamonds contain inclusions of graphite, hematite, and magnetite. These impurities scatter light, giving the diamond its signature deep-black appearance.

Their porous nature makes them tougher and more impact-resistant than traditional diamonds, which is why they are increasingly studied for industrial and technological applications.
