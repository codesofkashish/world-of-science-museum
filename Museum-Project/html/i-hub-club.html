I-HUB Club

A Collaborative Space for Innovation, Learning & Scientific Curiosity

About the I-HUB Club

The I-HUB Club is a dedicated innovation and learning community established under the Museum of Science to nurture creativity, scientific thinking, and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Designed for students, educators, researchers, and young innovators, the club acts as a bridge between scientific knowledge and real-world application.

Vision & Purpose

The core vision of the I-HUB Club is to create an ecosystem where ideas are encouraged, experimentation is supported, and learning extends beyond textbooks.

By providing access to resources, mentorship, and collaborative opportunities, the club empowers members to explore science, technology, and innovation in meaningful ways.

Membership & Participation

Membership to the I-HUB Club is open to school students, college students, educators, and early-career professionals with a passion for science and innovation.

Members gain opportunities to participate in exclusive workshops, access curated resources, and collaborate on interdisciplinary projects within a professional and inclusive environment.

Impact & Learning Outcomes

Through the I-HUB Club, participants develop critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and an appreciation for scientific inquiry.

The club plays a key role in nurturing future scientists, engineers, and innovators by offering exposure to real-world challenges and encouraging responsible innovation.

Looking Ahead

The I-HUB Club aims to expand its initiatives through partnerships with academic institutions, research organizations, and industry experts.

By fostering collaboration and continuous learning, the club envisions becoming a recognized platform for innovation-led education within the museum ecosystem.
